Deep Dive: Data Analysis

The Peregrine Falcon uses gravity as its primary weapon, reaching speeds faster than a race car during its dive. This high-speed "stoop" requires unique physiology, such as specialized nostrils to breathe at high velocities.

Conversely, the Bald and Golden Eagles focus on lift capacity. Their large wingspans allow them to soar for hours with minimal energy. While not as fast as the falcon, their ability to carry heavy prey is their most defining physical statistic.

Finally, the Barn Owl relies on a different statistic: silence. Their feathers are designed to break up air turbulence, making their flight nearly 100% silent.

Details and bird data was sourced from the Peregrine Fund Guide.
